Medical Database

PubMed

PubMed is a free database including primarily the MEDLINE database of references and abstracts on life sciences and biomedical topics.

It provides users with access to more than 30 million scientific articles in the field of medicine, biology, biochemistry and related disciplines.

EMBASE (Excerta Medica)

EMBASE is a database focused on medical research, pharmacology and biomedical aspects. Created by Elsevier, EMBASE offers a wide coverage of publications including journals, conferences and patents. The database is known for its special expert indexing and extensive terminology, which allows you to more accurately find research in specific fields of medicine.

MEDLINE

MEDLINE is the National Library of Medicine’s (NLM) premier bibliographic database that contains more than 31 million references to journal articles in life sciences with a concentration on biomedicine.

MEDLINE is a primary component of PubMed, a literature database developed and maintained by the NLM National Center for Biotechnology Information (NCBI). MEDLINE is the online counterpart to the MEDical Literature Analysis and Retrieval System (MEDLARS) that originated in 1964 (see MEDLINE history). A distinctive feature of MEDLINE is that the records are indexed with NLM Medical Subject Headings (MeSH).
