Infrastructure

Infrastructure refers to the fundamental facilities and systems that a country or region needs to function. This includes essential services like roads, bridges, dams, water and sewer systems, railways, airports, and harbors. These are typically government-built and publicly owned.

Environmental infrastructure specifically focuses on providing water supply, waste disposal, and pollution control services. This encompasses networks of aqueducts, reservoirs, pipes, treatment systems, and waste disposal facilities, all designed to protect human health and environmental quality.

The concept of infrastructure also extends to transportation networks, which are crucial for economic development by facilitating communication and commerce. Advances in civil engineering are introducing innovative materials and methods, such as mycelium bricks, self-healing concrete, and engineered wood, to create more sustainable and efficient infrastructure. Furthermore, digital tools like CAD and GIS, along with prefabrication and robotics, are transforming how infrastructure is planned, built, and managed.
